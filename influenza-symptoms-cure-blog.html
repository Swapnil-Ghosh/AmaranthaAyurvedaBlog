Don’t let Influenza spoil your Monsoon!

17 June 2017 Blog

The monsoons bring with them the much awaited rain, cool breeze and greenery. However it also brings in a host of viral infections: the most common among these being Influenza, better known as the ‘flu’. Influenza is a highly contagious respiratory infection caused by several flu viruses. Even though it is similar to the common cold, its symptoms are more severe and pronounced.

CAUSES

The flu virus travels through the air in the form of droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes or talks. These germs can be inhaled directly or can be picked up from objects- such as telephones, computer keyboards, and then be transferred into the eyes, nose or mouth.

Influenza is a contagious disease and the people who suffer from the virus are likely to be carriers from a day or so before the symptoms manifest, till about 5 days after they set in. Some people can be carriers for as long as 10 days after the symptoms appear while children and people with weakened immune systems may be carriers for a slightly longer period of time.

Antibodies against flu viruses are definitely helpful but cannot protect the patient from new influenza subtypes, which can be very different immunologically from a previous bout of influenza.

SYMPTOMS

● Fever over 100.4°F (38°C)s
● Aching muscles, especially in your back, arms and legs
● Chills and sweats
● Headache
● Dry, persistent coughs
● Fatigue and weakness
● Nasal congestion
● Sore throat
