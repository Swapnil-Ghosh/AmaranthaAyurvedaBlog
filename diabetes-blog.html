Diabetes and the role of Ayurveda in its management

02 June 2017 Blog

The current medical understanding of diabetes mellitus is that it is a disease in which the body’s ability to produce or respond to the hormone insulin, is impaired, resulting in abnormal metabolism of carbohydrates and elevated levels of glucose in the blood.

In simple language, glucose provides our body and mind with vital energy. It is the main source of energy for our cells and the main source of fuel for our brain. However, just like most things, too much of it can make one feel uneasy.

Diabetes can mainly be attributed to “malfunctioning” pancreas. Located behind the stomach, the pancreas releases hormones into the digestive system. When blood sugar levels get too high, beta cells in the pancreas release insulin- a hormone which causes cells to take in sugar, to be used as energy or to be stored as fat. This causes blood sugar levels to go back down.

Diabetes broken down

The Different types of Diabetes Mellitus are:

● Type 1 Diabetes
● Type 2 Diabetes
● Gestational Diabetes

TYPE 1

Type 1 diabetes or insulin dependent diabetes, often begins in childhood. It is an autoimmune condition in which the body attacks the pancreas with antibodies, preventing the production of insulin. This type of diabetes comes with a number of health risks like damages to the tiny blood vessels in the eyes, nerves and kidneys.

TYPE 2

Type 2 diabetes is the most common form, accounting for 95% of cases in adults. This type is also known as non insulin dependent diabetes and is prevalent mostly in adults.
In the case of Type 2 diabetes, the pancreas usually produce some insulin, but the quantity is either not enough for the body's needs, or the body's cells are resistant to it.
People who suffer from obesity (20% more than their ideal body weight for their height) are at a higher risk of developing Type 2 diabetes and its associated medical problems, as obesity leads to resistance to insulin. With insulin resistance, the pancreas has to work overtime to produce more insulin.

Common symptoms of type 2 diabetes are:

● Increased thirst
● Increased frequency of urination
● Hunger
● Fatigue
● Blurred vision

Conventional treatment focuses on keeping the blood sugar level steady through the use of ‘oral hypoglycemics’(OHAs), and through a carefully monitored diet and exercise. These measures, in turn minimize damage to the nerves, blood vessels and organs.

GESTATIONAL DIABETES

Gestational Diabetes can be referred to the type of diabetes triggered by pregnancy. It is often diagnosed in middle or late pregnancy. In this form of diabetes, high blood sugar levels in a mother are circulated though the placenta to the baby. Even though this form of diabetes resolves itself after pregnancy, it does put a mother at risk for developing type 2 diabetes in the future.

t is important to know that certain genetic mutations, hormonal diseases and variations, damage to or removal of the pancreas and medication which causes imbalance of insulin, can also put you at risk of getting diabetes.

Why go with Ayurveda:

In Ayurveda, Diabetes Mellitus is known as ‘Madhumeha‘(Madhu means honey and Meha means urine).

Madhumeha is classified as a Maha Rog (Major Disease) because, if not treated in time, it can lead to several complications in the body. Ayurvedic treatment is known to be helpful in managing diabetes and when used in conjunction with OHAs, studies show that the blood sugar levels stay stable over longer periods of time. The combined therapy also prevents the desensitization of the beta cells to the OHAs, thus delaying increase in the OHA dosage.

Ayurvedic medicines are made from a combination of natural herbs and ingredients, which have properties that have a long documented role in the management of diabetes. They may even be used for ‘pre-diabetic’ individuals (those who are at a higher risk of getting diabetes due to genetic or lifestyle factors but are as yet, undiagnosed) in order to delay/prevent onset. Ayurveda aims at rejuvenating the body to not only balance body sugar level, but also ensure that the complications normally associated with this disease are minimized.
